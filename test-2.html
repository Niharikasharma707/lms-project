1) The stopping potential value is 0.6 V when the light source is kept at a distance of 20 cm. When the source is kept at 40 cm away, the stopping potential will be -

a)0.6 V   b)0.3 V   c)1.2 V   d)2.4 V

2) The minimum energy required to remove an electron is called -

a)Stopping potential   b)Kinetic energy   c)Work function   d)None of these

3) What is the value of the maximum kinetic energy acquired by electron due to radiation of wavelength 100 nm? -

a)12 eV   b)6.2 eV   c)100 eV   d)300 eV

4) The work function of lithium is 2.5 eV. The maximum wavelength of light that can cause the photoelectric effect in lithium is -

a)3980 Å   b)4980 Å   c)5980 Å   d)6980 Å

Solutions :

1) a 2) c 3) b 4) b
